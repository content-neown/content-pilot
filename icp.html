👤 Who She Is
Age & Life Stage
A mother aged 30–42, with a child between 5 and 12 (sometimes as young as 3). Not defined by work status — the split between working mothers and stay-at-home parents is almost even.
Location
Mumbai dominates (25 of 49), but nearly half are from outside — Hyderabad, Delhi, Ahmedabad, Bhopal, Bhubaneswar, Salem, Ranchi, and 8+ more Tier 2 cities.
Spending Behaviour
34 of 49 spend under ₹5,000/month on all child activities. neOwn is not a luxury purchase for her — it's a smart one. Budget-aware, not budget-limited.
Parenting Style
Structured but not controlling. She plans academics, gives autonomy on extracurriculars, researches purchases, and is drawn to services that make her feel like she's doing the right thing.
The Core Tension
She has a conviction that books are foundational, but she didn't grow up reading for pleasure herself. She can't sit beside her child and model the habit. What she has is aspiration — and what neOwn gives her is a system to act on it without having to be the example.
😟 Her Anxiety Stack — Two Layers
🔝 Surface Anxiety

My child is too distracted and screen-dependent

She sees her child gravitating toward passive, mindless consumption. Books feel like the antidote. This is what she talks about — the visible, concrete problem she can point to.

30/49 cite screen time
12/49 cite low attention span
Same fear, two words
🔻 Deeper Anxiety

I'm not doing enough for my child

She believes reading is foundational. She didn't read as a child. She can't model the habit at home. A quiet, persistent guilt that her child is missing something important — and that it's partly on her.

Doesn't show in checkboxes
Lives in open-text responses
Drives purchase psychology
Why neOwn resolves both: It addresses the surface anxiety practically — books replace screens. And it addresses the deeper anxiety emotionally — subscribing is proof to herself that she took action. The act of subscribing is itself relieving. When her child enjoys the books, it quiets both anxieties at once.

📚 What Actually Keeps Children Engaged
🎯
Child-Led Selection
When children choose their own books, they read them. The app's selection experience isn't just UX — it's the core engagement mechanism. Parents say it again and again.
📦
Monthly Delivery as Event
The anticipation of new books arriving every month is a motivation engine. "I am happy to see the excitement in my child every month when neOwn delivers books."
🏆
Challenges & Rewards
Programs like the 20x20 challenge actively extend the subscriber lifecycle. A goal and a finish line give children a reason to engage beyond just reading a book.
